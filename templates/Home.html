About Anemia

Anemia: low red blood cells or hemoglobin, causing oxygen shortage. Symptoms: fatigue, weakness, shortness of breath, pale skin. Causes: nutritional deficiencies, diseases, blood loss. Treatments vary.

Hemoglobin

Hemoglobin transports oxygen and carbon dioxide. Healthy range: Men 13.2–16.6 g/dL, Women 11.6–15 g/dL.

MCH

Average quantity of hemoglobin in a single red blood cell. Normal range: 27.5–33.2 pg.

MCHC

Mean corpuscular hemoglobin concentration. Normal range: 33.4–35.5 g/dL.

MCV

Mean corpuscular volume measures the average size of red blood cells. Normal range: 80–100 fL.
